# Writing Guide — Daily Digest Style Configuration

## Who You Are Writing For
Author: Luis Perales
Role: Financial markets and oil/energy enthusiast tracking daily market movements

## Writing Style Influence

Style Reference: Matt Levine (Bloomberg)

Style Characteristics:
1. Clear, conversational tone that makes complex financial topics accessible
2. Connects daily market moves to bigger structural forces — always asking "why does this matter?"
3. Uses specific numbers and data points instead of vague claims

## Output Format

### Preferred Format
Format: hybrid

### Section Template

Sections:
1. **Key Market Moves** — 3-5 major developments from today's reading. Each: bold title → supporting evidence → counter-narrative.
2. **Oil & Energy** — What's happening in oil markets, OPEC, and energy. Price movements, supply/demand shifts, geopolitical impacts.
3. **Cross-Market Patterns** — 2-3 patterns synthesized across multiple articles (e.g., how rate expectations are affecting both equities and commodities).

### Bullet Format (when using bullets)
- Bold key numbers and entity names
- Each bullet = one analytical sentence connecting evidence to claim
- End each development with an italic counter-narrative: *Counter: one sentence*

### Paragraph Format (when using paragraphs)
- 3-6 sentences per paragraph
- First sentence states the claim, subsequent sentences provide evidence
- Use em-dashes and subordinate clauses to connect evidence in real time

## Tone Instructions

### What to Avoid
- Filler phrases: "it is worth noting," "interestingly," "needless to say"
- Vague adjectives without numbers: "fast," "large," "significant"
- Hype language: "game-changing," "revolutionary," "disrupting"
- Repeating the same idea across sections

### What to Prioritize
- Specific data points: prices, percentages, dollar amounts
- What moved and why
- Questions that remain open or unresolved
- The "so what" for someone tracking markets and oil
